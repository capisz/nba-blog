# Knicks-Spurs Game 1 Report: The Comeback That Changed the Finals

The Finals opener looked like it was getting away from the Knicks before it became the kind of win that can travel with a team for the rest of a series.

San Antonio had the first big push. The Spurs led by 14 in the second half, protected the paint with size, and made New York work through every half-court possession. For a while, the Knicks looked like a team trying to figure out the Finals in real time.

Then the game changed. New York tightened the defense, got cleaner possessions from its guards, and turned the final stretch into a test of poise. The Knicks won 105-95 and stole home-court advantage before the series had time to settle.

## What decided it

This was not about one giant scoring burst as much as it was about the Knicks refusing to let the game stay on San Antonio's terms.

The Spurs' length made the floor feel crowded early. Once New York started moving the ball earlier and attacking before the defense was fully set, the same pressure that had slowed the Knicks down started creating gaps. The comeback was built on patience, but it was finished with force.

## Why it mattered

Game 1 gave the Knicks permission to believe their identity could hold up in the Finals.

Winning in San Antonio matters. Winning after trailing by double digits matters more. The Knicks did not just take the opener. They showed that even when Wembanyama and the Spurs made the game awkward, New York had enough counters to survive the first problem and find the next one.

## Postgame interview note

Brunson's postgame answer matched the shape of the comeback. He did not sell the night as magic or destiny. In NBA.com's post-Game 1 media recap, he talked about controlling what he could control, moving forward possession by possession, and responding in a positive way when the game did not start cleanly.

That is the real summary of Game 1. The Knicks were not smooth for most of the night, but their captain's answer was the same as his fourth quarter: do not chase the whole game at once, win the next possession, and let the pressure move back to San Antonio.

## Thread note

Final: Knicks 105, Spurs 95.

Series: Knicks lead 1-0.
