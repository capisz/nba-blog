# Knicks-Spurs Game 2 Report: The One-Point Escape

Game 2 was the kind of win that makes a series feel both better and more dangerous.

The Knicks beat the Spurs 105-104, took a 2-0 Finals lead, and left San Antonio with exactly what they came for. But this was not a clean getaway. It was a one-point game that stayed tense until the end, the kind of night where every missed rotation and every empty possession feels like it might become the thing everyone talks about for two days.

New York survived anyway.

## The game never opened up

After Game 1's comeback, the question was whether the Knicks could make the next game simpler. They could not. San Antonio made them work again, and the margins were tiny from start to finish.

That is what made the win important. The Knicks did not need to play a perfect road game to win a Finals game. They needed enough shot-making, enough stops, and enough calm late to keep the Spurs from turning the series before it reached Madison Square Garden.

## The pressure shifts

A 2-0 lead is not the end of a Finals series, especially against a team with Victor Wembanyama. But it changes the emotional math. Suddenly the Spurs were the team chasing. Suddenly every possession in Game 3 would carry the weight of San Antonio trying to avoid a hole that might become too deep.

That is what the Knicks earned in Game 2: not comfort, but control.

## Postgame interview note

Towns' ESPN interview gave the box score a different emotional weight. After a 21-point, 13-rebound night, he dedicated the win to his late mother and talked like a player who understood the game was bigger than a clean stat line.

That answer belongs in the recap because Game 2 was not only about the Knicks surviving Wembanyama's final look. It was about Towns giving New York the kind of frontcourt calm it needed while the game got tight, then carrying the moment with a personal edge after the horn.
