# Knicks-Spurs Game 2 Postgame: The One-Point Escape

## Episode Summary

The Knicks beat the Spurs 105-104 in Game 2, which is both exactly what they wanted and exactly the kind of score that keeps everyone awake afterward. New York left San Antonio up 2-0, but the narrow finish made it clear the series was still full of pressure points.

## Key Topics

- Why a one-point road win can be more revealing than a comfortable win
- How the Knicks survived the late-game pressure
- What a 2-0 Finals lead changes before the series moves to MSG
- Why San Antonio still had a path back into the series

## Show Notes

This episode is about the emotional swing of Game 2. The Knicks got the result, but the Spurs kept the margin tight enough to make every possession feel important. The win gave New York control of the series, not permission to relax.

## Transcript

[00:00] The Knicks are up 2-0 in the NBA Finals, and somehow Game 2 still felt like a warning. That is what a 105-104 road win does.

[10:20] The biggest thing New York earned was control. They took both games in San Antonio and made the Spurs chase the series before it even reached Madison Square Garden.

[26:40] The warning is that a one-point game can flip on one possession. The Knicks got away with enough mistakes in Game 2. They cannot assume that will keep happening.
