# Knicks-Spurs Game 5 Report: The Championship Closeout

The Knicks did not make the last step easy, because nothing about this run was ever built to be easy.

San Antonio had the home floor, the early force, and the exact kind of defensive pressure that can make a closeout game start to feel heavier by the minute. The Spurs led by 16 in the first half and still had a double-digit cushion in the fourth quarter. For most teams, that is where the road title dream starts leaking air.

The Knicks turned it into one more comeback.

New York beat San Antonio 94-90, won the Finals 4-1, and claimed its first NBA championship since 1973. It was the kind of final score that tells you how tight the night felt, but not how big the moment became. The Knicks had to win through cold shooting, foul trouble, Wembanyama's rim protection, and a Spurs team trying to drag the series back to New York.

Jalen Brunson would not let it happen.

## What decided it

Game 5 belonged to Brunson because the Knicks needed one player to bend the night back toward them.

He scored 45 points, set the franchise's Finals scoring record, and carried the fourth quarter when the offense was running on nerve more than rhythm. The Spurs kept making the game ugly. Brunson kept finding a cleaner answer than everyone else.

That was the difference between pressure and panic.

The Spurs had the length. Victor Wembanyama finished with 19 points, 14 rebounds, and five blocks. Dylan Harper gave San Antonio 25 points and kept the home team alive. But when the game became possessions instead of quarters, New York had the player who could keep getting to the next shot.

Brunson's late floater with 1:05 left gave the Knicks the lead they needed. From there, the defense and the foul line had to do the rest.

## Why it mattered

This was not just a closeout. It was a release.

The Knicks had waited 53 years for a championship. They had lost Finals, lost eras, lost credibility, lost patience, and lost entire generations of fans to the feeling that the next real title team might never arrive.

Then this group ended it in San Antonio.

The series finished in five games, but the margins were never comfortable. Game 2 was one point. Game 4 was one point and the largest comeback in Finals history. Game 5 was four points, with the Spurs still hunting a tying shot in the final seconds. That is why the title feels less like a clean march and more like a refusal.

The Knicks kept surviving the moment until there were no more moments left to survive.

## Postgame interview note

The postgame answers sounded like the whole run.

Brunson was named Finals MVP after the closeout and talked like a player still processing the size of it. NBA.com's live recap noted that he said he was in awe, then pointed back to the team's habit of finding a way whenever something was put in front of them.

That is the Game 5 summary. The Spurs put a 16-point lead in front of them. They put Wembanyama at the rim. They put a road closeout on the board. The Knicks found a way anyway.
