# Knicks-Spurs Game 1 Postgame: The Comeback Opener

## Episode Summary

The Knicks opened the Finals with a 105-95 win in San Antonio, and the story was not just the final score. It was the climb back from 14 down, the way New York kept solving the floor, and the feeling that this series would not be decided by who looked better in the first quarter.

## Key Topics

- How the Knicks turned a 14-point deficit into a road win
- Why San Antonio's length bothered New York early
- The late-game poise that stole home-court advantage
- What Game 1 revealed about the matchup

## Show Notes

This episode focuses on how New York found its rhythm after the Spurs controlled the early shape of the game. The win gave the Knicks a 1-0 series lead and forced San Antonio to respond immediately in Game 2.

## Transcript

[00:00] The Knicks took Game 1, and they did it the hard way. Down 14 in the second half, on the road, in the NBA Finals, against a Spurs team that made the floor feel small early.

[12:30] The important thing is not just that New York came back. It is that the Knicks found answers without abandoning who they are.

[27:45] Game 1 does not win the series, but it changes the pressure. The Spurs now have to respond to the Knicks, not the other way around.
